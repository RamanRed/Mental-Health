It provides structured data for the LLM screening module and for the doctor's pre-consultation brief. Patients are encouraged to complete it progressively — not all at once. How would you rate your overall quality of life right now?How satisfied are you with your health and overall sense of wellbeing?In the past month, have you felt that things are going wrong and there is no way out?Domain 2 — Psychological HealthHow often have you been feeling sad, hopeless, or emotionally low in the past month?How much do you feel your life has meaning and purpose?Have you had thoughts of harming yourself or ending your life in the past month? (sensitive)Domain 3 — Trauma & Past ExperiencesHave you ever experienced a traumatic event that still affects you today? (e.g. abuse, violence, sudden loss, accident)Did you grow up in a household where there was frequent conflict, addiction, or instability?Are there specific situations, people, or memories that trigger strong fear or distress in you?Do you find yourself avoiding certain places, topics, or memories because they feel too painful?Domain 4 — Insecurities & Self-PerceptionAre there aspects of yourself — your appearance, personality, or past — that you feel deeply ashamed or insecure about?How often do you feel "not good enough" or harshly judged by others?Domain 5 — Physical HealthHow satisfied are you with your sleep quality?How often have you felt persistently tired or lacking energy for no clear reason in the past month?Domain 6 — Social Relationships & TrustHow satisfied are you with your personal relationships (family, friends, partner)?Do you find it difficult to trust people, even those close to you?When you are going through something painful, do you open up to others or keep it to yourself?Domain 7 — Environment & SafetyHow safe do you feel in your home and daily environment?Have you experienced a major stressful life event in the past year? (e.g. loss, breakup, job change, illness)Domain 8 — Coping & Emotional RegulationWhen you feel overwhelmed, what do you typically do to cope? It is NOT a clinical diagnosis. It is a triage and prioritisation layer. All high-risk flags must be reviewed and confirmed by a doctor before any action is taken.The LLM module aggregates inputs from questionnaire scores, mood journal trends, streak engagement patterns, audio session notes (transcribed), and demographic context. It produces a risk classification on three levels:Normal / Low RiskPeriodic monitoring recommended. No immediate escalation. Streak encouragement continues.Moderate RiskEarly intervention suggested. Guardian notified (subject to patient consent). Doctor review queued.High Risk / Trauma IndicatorImmediate escalation to doctor.if doctor not available then Guardian 1 + ASHA worker alerted. Pre-consultation brief auto-generated.The LLM module runs on the following schedule:Weekly scheduled run for all active patientsOn-demand trigger: significant change in mood score, PHQ-9 / GAD-7 result, or streak drop-offPre-consultation trigger: always run fresh before any scheduled doctor sessionExplainability: the doctor receives a plain-language summary of which factors contributed to the risk classification. This prevents 'black box' usage in clinical contexts.2.6 Consultation Visibility for PatientPatient can view all consultation summaries stored in the databasePatient controls guardian access via consent toggles in settingsPatient receives notifications for upcoming follow-up appointmentsPatient can revoke guardian access at any time with doctor approval — takes immediate effect3. Guardian (User Type 2) — Extended Mechanics3.1 Who Can Be a Guardian?The guardian role is designed for the Indian grassroots health ecosystem. The following individuals can register as a guardian :CategoryTypical LiteracyPrimary Use CaseFamily member (most literate in household)Moderate to HighDaily observation, close care, doctor communication on behalf of patientNGO / NSS VolunteerModerate to HighCommunity-level support, patient onboarding in underserved areasASHA Worker (Accredited Social Health Activist)ModerateRegional monitoring across multiple patients; routine check-insAnganwadi WorkerModerateGrassroots women and child mental health; rural community entry point3.2 Guardian Roles by Patient Literacy LevelThe guardian's functional role changes based on the literacy level of the patient they are supporting:3.2.1 For Literate Patients — Guardian as Consultant / ObserverDoctor may optionally loop in the guardian for care coordination discussionsGuardian sees only what patient and doctor explicitly share (consent-gated)Doctor may discuss care plans with guardian if patient has granted consentGuardian cannot override or edit patient-submitted dataPrimary communication is between doctor and patient; guardian is secondary3.2.2 For Illiterate / Low-Literacy Patients — Guardian as Primary InterfaceGuardian registers the patient and fills the identity form on their behalfGuardian answers the questionnaire based on their own direct observation of the patientGuardian provides voice or written inputs during follow-up sessionsGuardian receives guidance and instructions from the doctor throughout sessionsASHA worker acts as the bridge between the patient household and the formal health systemAll guardian-submitted data is attributed with filled_by = guardian_id in the database to maintain data provenance3.3 Dual Guardian System — Detailed MechanicsThe dual-guardian design solves a real operational problem: ASHA workers cover large geographic regions with many patients. Guardian 1 (family) provides the close daily observation, while Guardian 2 (ASHA/NGO) monitors across a wider cohort. This division reduces duplication of effort and enables efficient regional-level monitoring.3.3.1 Guardian 1 — Primary Caregiver (Family)Typically the most literate adult family member: spouse, parent, or adult siblingHas physical proximity to the patient — ideally shares or frequently visits the householdProvides daily or near-daily observation inputsAccessible to patient at any time for support; serves as first point of contact in non-emergency situationsWe are storing the conatact details of guardian 1 at the time of form filing which will be used as a primary contact to the family member kinda guardians3.3.2 Guardian 2 — Community Health Worker (ASHA / NGO) only this guardian will have a dashbaorad on our platform to monitor multiple patients in a region providing assistance Manages a geographic cohort of multiple patients in their assigned regionPerforms routine scheduled check-ins across all assigned patientsViews Guardian 1 updates to reduce duplication — only follows up on gaps or anomaliesIn the absence of a Guardian 1, takes on full guardian responsibilities for that patientAccess is read-only for routine check-in data; clinical summaries require mutual consent from patient and doctor3.3.3 Single Guardian ScenariosSome patients will have only one guardian. The following cases are handled:Only an ASHA worker is availableASHA worker acts as the sole guardian. They have both the close-observation responsibilities of Guardian 1 and the monitoring responsibilities of Guardian 2.Only a family member is availableFamily member acts as sole Guardian 1. ASHA worker visibility is not available unless the patient later links one.No guardian availablePatient proceeds without a guardian. AI screening still runs. Doctor is the primary point of escalation.3.4 Guardian Permissions MatrixPermissionGuardian 1 (Family)Guardian 2 (ASHA / NGO)View patient identity profileYes (registrant if illiterate patient)Yes — own region onlyView questionnaire responsesWith patient or doctor consentWith patient or doctor consentView consultation summariesMutual consent: patient AND doctorRead-only; only if both patient and doctor allowView mood journal / streakWith patient consentRoutine check-in data onlyAdd observations / notesYes — submitted for doctor reviewYes — submitted for doctor reviewReceive alerts / notificationsWith patient consentHigh-risk patient alerts onlySee other patientsNo — single patient onlyYes — only patients in assigned regionEdit patient-submitted dataNoNo3.5 Guardian Access Dispute ResolutionPatient consent always takes precedence over doctor recommendation. Doctor (User Type 3) — Session Pipeline & AI Integration4.1 Role OverviewThe doctor is the clinical authority on the platform. Their workflow is structured in three layers: early screening, online follow-up sessions, and AI-assisted form filling. The doctor also manages guardian access rights in partnership with the patient.4.2 Pre-Consultation — Early ScreeningThe first interaction with the doctor is framed as an early screening session, not a full consultation. This lowers barriers for patients unfamiliar with mental health services and optimises doctor time by filtering cases before full clinical engagement.Doctor reviews the AI screening (basis of streak data) output and mood/questionnaire history before the sessionScreening session can be conducted online (audio/video) or at a community health centre in personSession is recorded with explicit patient consent — recording is required for the transcription pipelineBased on screening outcome, doctor decides whether further follow-up sessions are needed4.3 Online Follow-Up Session StructureAfter the initial screening, up to 2–3 online follow-up consultations . Could be upto 2 or 3 sessins according ti doctor . This keeps the system lean while covering the most critical early-intervention windowSessionFormatKey Doctor ActionsSession 1 — Early ScreeningOnline audio/video or in-personReview AI flags, assess patient directly, decide care pathway (monitor / follow-up / escalate to offline specialist)Session 2 — Follow-Up 1Online (audio/video)Review mood and trait progress since screening, update care notes, adjust plan if neededSession 3 — Follow-Up 2Online (audio/video)Reassess trajectory. Close case, continue monitoring, or escalate to offline/specialist care4.4 AI-Assisted Form Filling Pipeline (Post-Session)This is a core differentiating feature of MANAS. After each session, the audio transcript is processed by an LLM against predefined clinical checkbox forms. The doctor validates — not manually fills — the form. This reduces documentation burden by an estimated 60–70%.The post-session pipeline runs in the following sequence:Step 1 — Audio CaptureSession audio is captured. Consent gate: recording only starts if patient has granted audio consent in their settings.Step 2 — Speech-to-Text TranscriptionAudio is transcribed using Groq api as fallback for regional language sessions. Transcription is stored as raw text linked to the consultation record.Step 3 — LLM Form Auto-FillLLM processes the transcript against predefined clinical forms present in the prompt which I will be passing (checkbox / structured field format). Output is structured JSON matching the form schema.Step 4 — Doctor ValidationDoctor reviews the auto-filled form. Low-confidence fields are highlighted. Safety-critical fields (suicidal ideation, self-harm) are NEVER auto-filled — always require manual entry. Make some changes if he/she wantrsStep 5 — Consultation Summary GenerationA plain-language summary of the session is generated from the validated form and transcript. Doctor can edit before finalising.Step 6 — Access ControlDoctor selects which guardian(s) can view the consultation summary. Patient must confirm or deny this access. Summary is then stored in the patient's database record.4.5 Clinical Form SchemaClinical forms are stored in the clinical_forms table as JSON schema definitions. Each form has versioning. The doctor selects which form template to apply in a given session. Forms consist of:Checkbox fields — for standardised symptom presence/absenceNumeric rating fields — for severity scalesFree-text fields — for qualitative clinical notes (not auto-filled)Safety flags — always manual; trigger alerts if positiveGuardian-accessible forms: the doctor can mark specific completed forms as accessible to a designated guardian. Patient consent is required for this access.4.6 Guardian Access Management by DoctorDoctor can nominate a specific guardian to receive consultation summaries after each sessionPatient must confirm or deny this access in their settings — it is not automaticAccess can be time-limited (e.g., for next 30 days only) or session-specificASHA worker (Guardian 2) access is always read-only and covers only routine check-in data, not clinical summaries, unless explicitly grantedDoctor and patient together decide whether consultation content is accessible to Guardian 1 and/or Guardian 25. Open Questions — Addendum ItemsThe following questions arise specifically from the extended ideation in v1.1 and supplement the Open Questions table in v1.0 Section 13:Can a Guardian 2 (ASHA) be linked to a patient without the patient explicitly consenting?Proposed: Yes, linkage by location is automatic. But ASHA can only see basic check-in data unless patient grants further consent.What happens if Guardian 1 (family) and Guardian 2 (ASHA) provide conflicting observations?Both inputs are stored independently. Doctor sees both with attribution. Doctor adjudicates. No automatic merging.How does a patient transition from guardian-assisted to self-managed over time?Literacy level is updatable. Patient can claim their own account, change consent toggles, and reduce guardian scope. Needs a verification step (doctor or counsellor sign-off recommended).Who approves a new ASHA worker / guardian registration?Proposed: district-level admin (NHM integration) or doctor master admin. Cannot be self-approved to prevent fraud.Can a guardian remove themselves from a patient's profile?Yes. Linkage is voluntary. Removal takes effect immediately. If no guardian remains, patient is flagged for doctor attention.How is audio consent handled for minors or patients with diminished capacity?Guardian provides consent on behalf. Must be explicitly recorded with guardian_id in the consent audit log.7.
